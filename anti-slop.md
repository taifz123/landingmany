# Anti-Slop Directive

This page must look like it was designed by a senior UI designer with a specific creative vision — not generated by an AI following a template. Every section should have at least one element that makes someone pause and think "that's well done."

## Banned Visual Patterns
These are the most common AI-generated landing page clichés. None of them may appear:

**Layout slop:**
- Three identical cards in a row with icon-title-description format and equal padding
- Centred everything — at least one section should use asymmetric or left-aligned layout
- Hero section that's just centred text with a gradient background and nothing else
- Sections that all look the same — vary the layout rhythm (full-width, cards, split, stacked)

**Colour slop:**
- Purple-to-blue, pink-to-purple, or any "tech startup" gradient
- White or light grey page backgrounds
- Using every brand colour in every section — exercise restraint, let sections breathe
- Neon or oversaturated accent colours

**Typography slop:**
- Inter, Roboto, Open Sans, Lato, Montserrat, Poppins as the primary display font
- ALL CAPS on body text (ALL CAPS is fine for small labels and badges only)
- Inconsistent font sizing — follow the type scale, don't eyeball it

**Copy slop (if generating any copy not in the reference file):**
- "Transform your business"
- "Take your [X] to the next level"
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "Unlock the power of"
- "Seamlessly integrate"
- "It's not just a [X], it's a [Y]"

**Component slop:**
- Testimonial sections with circular avatar placeholders and quotation mark icons
- "Trusted by" logo bars with grey placeholder rectangles
- Toggle switches for pricing (monthly/annual)
- Floating chat widget icons in the bottom right
- Cookie consent banners (not needed for a landing page with no tracking)

## What "Stunning" Actually Looks Like

**Depth and layering:** Elements should feel like they exist on different planes. Use layered box-shadows, subtle background patterns at different opacities, and z-index intentionally.

**Intentional asymmetry:** The hero should have a clear visual weight distribution — headline block on one side, iPhone mock on the other, not perfectly centred mirrored.

**Micro-details that reward attention:** A subtle gradient shift on the nav when scrolled. Chat bubbles in the iPhone mock that have slightly different border-radius on each corner (like real iOS messages). The typing indicator dots that animate with staggered timing. A thin gold line that traces the "How It Works" steps.

**Whitespace as a design element:** Sections should breathe. Don't fill every pixel. The space between the hero headline and the subhead matters. The padding around the case study card communicates confidence.

**Dark theme done right:** Dark themes fail when everything is the same shade. Use --dark for the base, --dark-mid for elevated surfaces, and subtle teal/gold glows to create visual hierarchy without relying on bright colours.
